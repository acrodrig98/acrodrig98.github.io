# Welcome to My Blog

I've been meaning to start a blog for a while, and I'm finally doing it. My goal is to use this space to write about astrophysics research in an accessible way, share updates on my work, and occasionally reflect on what it's like to be a researcher in this field.

## What to expect

Most posts will fall into one of a few categories:

- **Research updates** — highlights from recent papers (mine and others') in binary stars, X-ray astronomy, and gravitational-wave sources
- **Explainers** — accessible write-ups on topics I find fascinating, aimed at a general scientifically curious audience
- **Career & academia** — occasional thoughts on the academic research path, fellowships, writing, and advice I wish I'd had earlier

## A bit about my work right now

I'm currently a Harvard Prize Postdoctoral Fellow at the Center for Astrophysics, studying the extreme physics of compact binary stars. A lot of my work involves combing through enormous astronomical datasets—millions to billions of sources—looking for rare, exotic objects: magnetic white dwarfs, ultracompact binaries destined to merge, and an entirely new class of pulsating radio sources called Long Period Transients.

It's a strange and wonderful time to be in this field. New all-sky surveys like eROSITA and ZTF are opening entirely new windows on the universe, and discoveries that would have taken decades to accumulate before can now happen in a single observing season.

I'll write more about all of this soon. Thanks for stopping by.

— Tony
